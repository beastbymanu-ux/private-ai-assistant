ACME Corporation — Internal AI Usage Policy
Last Updated: January 2026
Classification: Internal

1. PURPOSE

This policy establishes guidelines for the use of artificial intelligence tools
and large language models within ACME Corporation operations. All employees
must comply with these guidelines when using AI in their work.

2. APPROVED AI TOOLS

The following AI tools are approved for internal use:
- ACME Internal Assistant (self-hosted, approved for all data classifications)
- Python-based automation scripts (approved by IT department)
- Internal data analysis tools running on company servers

The following tools are NOT approved for use with company data:
- ChatGPT (OpenAI) — data is sent to external servers
- Google Gemini — data may be used for training
- Any cloud-based AI service not listed above

3. DATA CLASSIFICATION AND AI

3.1 Confidential Data
Confidential data must NEVER be processed through external AI services.
This includes: customer records, financial data, employee information,
trade secrets, and any data subject to GDPR or other regulatory requirements.

3.2 Internal Data
Internal data may only be processed through self-hosted AI tools that
run entirely on ACME infrastructure.

3.3 Public Data
Public data may be processed through approved external tools with
manager approval.

4. COMPLIANCE REQUIREMENTS

4.1 GDPR Compliance
All AI processing of personal data must comply with GDPR Article 5
principles: lawfulness, fairness, transparency, purpose limitation,
data minimization, accuracy, storage limitation, and integrity.

4.2 Data Residency
All AI processing must occur within the EU. No data may be transferred
to servers outside the European Economic Area without explicit approval
from the Data Protection Officer.

4.3 Audit Trail
All AI-assisted decisions must maintain a complete audit trail,
including: input data, model used, output generated, and any
human review applied.

5. INCIDENT REPORTING

Any suspected data breach involving AI tools must be reported to the
IT Security team within 24 hours. This includes accidental submission
of confidential data to external AI services.

6. TRAINING REQUIREMENTS

All employees using AI tools must complete:
- AI Usage Policy Training (annual)
- Data Protection Basics (annual)
- Responsible AI Practices (upon onboarding)
